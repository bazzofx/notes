# Cyber Law

Cyber law, also known as Internet Law or Cyber Law, is the part of the overall legal system that is related to legal informatics and supervises the digital circulation of information, e-commerce, software, and information security. It is associated with legal informatics and electronic elements, including information systems, computers, software, and hardware.

## UK and European Laws:

Cyber laws in the UK and Europe are governed by various regulations and directives aimed at ensuring cybersecurity, data protection, and the prevention of cybercrimes. Key legislation includes:

### **United Kingdom (UK) Cyber Laws:**

- **Computer Misuse Act 1990**  
  - Unauthorized access to computer material (hacking).  
  - Unauthorized access with intent to commit further offenses.  
  - Unauthorized acts with intent to impair the operation of a computer.  
  - Making, supplying, or obtaining tools for cyber offenses.  

- **Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018)**  
  - Implements the UKâ€™s version of the **General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)**.  
  - Regulates the processing of personal data.  
  - Protects individuals' rights regarding their data.  

- **Network and Information Systems Regulations 2018 (NIS Regulations)**  
  - Aims to improve cybersecurity in essential services and digital service providers.  
  - Mandates incident reporting and security measures for operators of essential services.  

- **Online Safety Act 2023**  
  - Aims to regulate harmful online content.  
  - Imposes responsibilities on social media and tech platforms to prevent illegal content.  

- **Cybersecurity Strategy 2022-2030**  
  - Provides a framework for national cyber resilience and protection against cyber threats.  

### **European Union (EU) Cyber Laws:**

- **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (Regulation (EU) 2016/679)**  
  - Sets strict rules for the collection, storage, and processing of personal data.  
  - Grants individuals rights over their data, including the right to access, correct, and delete data.  
  - Requires businesses to implement strong cybersecurity measures.  

- **Directive on Security of Network and Information Systems (NIS2 Directive) (EU 2022/2555)**  
  - Strengthens cybersecurity across critical sectors such as energy, transport, banking, and healthcare.  
  - Expands security obligations for essential and important entities.  

- **EU Cybersecurity Act (Regulation (EU) 2019/881)**  
  - Establishes the European Union Agency for Cybersecurity (ENISA).  
  - Introduces a cybersecurity certification framework for ICT products and services.  

- **ePrivacy Directive (Directive 2002/58/EC, updated as the ePrivacy Regulation - upcoming)**  
  - Regulates electronic communications, cookies, and digital marketing privacy.  

- **Digital Services Act (DSA) & Digital Markets Act (DMA) (2022)**  
  - Regulates online platforms, ensuring transparency and accountability in handling digital content.  
  - Imposes obligations on large tech firms regarding competition and fair digital markets.  

These laws form the foundation of cybersecurity, data protection, and online safety regulations in the UK and Europe, ensuring individuals' rights and business responsibilities in the digital world.
